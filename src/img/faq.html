Create or Delete Accounts

How to Delete a Grant Us Funding account:

Deleting your Grant Us Funding account deletes all of your data from GrantUsFunding.com. When you delete your Grant Us Funding account:

  • You will not be able to access your account once it’s canceled.
  • If you downloaded files from us, they will stay on your computer.
  • Your files are deleted from Grant Us Funding servers and will not be retrievable.

Grant Us Funding Users/Team Admins

  1. Login to your portal at GrantUsFunding.com.
  2. Scroll down to the Admin section.
  3. Under Admin, check Delete Account.
  4. Then check the save button.

If you delete your account, we will initiate permanent deletion of the Grant Us Funding files you stored on Grant Us Funding after ~30 days. If you mark a file within your account for permanent deletion, it will be permanently deleted within ~60 days.

Important note: Deleting an account can’t be undone. Even if you restart service, your data may be permanently deleted and irretrievable. We will assist you in all ways possible if the deletion was a mistake, and try, without a guarantee, to retrieve and reestablish your account data.

Grant Us Funding Team Members

If you’re a Grant Us Funding Team Member, you can’t delete accounts or cancel your subscription. If you’re a Team Member, and you’d like to delete your account, contact your Team Admin.

Plans & Upgrades

In our Education Pathway, we offer two options: the Essential Start and Enterprise plans. Each has its own set of options, which can be seen in our Plans & Pricing page.

At Grant Us Funding we recognize that many of you will have different needs. We can offer a custom set of plan options. Simply drop us a line at support@GrantUsFunding.com.

Upgrades: Your Admin can make a change between the two plans in their portal. Security & Privacy

Secure your Grant Us Funding Account

A password is always necessary to connect to a Grant Us Funding account. Your password may be recorded in your browser or password program if you gave it permission.

It’s also possible that someone has access to your email and password. If you see activity on your account that you don’t recognize, secure your Grant Us Funding account by following these steps:

  1. Login to your portal.
  2. Change your password immediately.
  3. If you can’t access your account because the message you receive indicates a mismathc of our password, go to the reset password button. You should receive an email with further instruction. If you don’t receive it, please check your Spam folder before contacting us. If you’re unable to reset your password and gain reentry into your account, please contact us at service@GrantUsFunding.com and let us know ASAP.
  4. If you can access your account, check that your data is intact.
  5. When in your portal, for extra security you may want to change the email settings in addition to your password.

If you receive a password or email change notification, but didn't reset it yourself:


If you unexpectedly received a password or email change notification, first verify that the email really came from Grant Us Funding. To be safe, don't click any links in the email—there's always a chance that the email is a fake, sent by phishers trying to access your Grant Us Funding account.

Instead, go to Grant Us Funding.com and see if you can sign in to your account using your original password. (You may need to sign out first if you're already signed in).If you can sign in, then your password was never changed, and you should ignore that (likely) fake email. Forward the entire email to abuse@GrantUsFunding.com and we'll investigate.

If you can't sign in, then you should first go to the Grant Us Funding login and click the Forgot your password button. Try to reset your Grant Us Funding password as soon as possible. Use the link in an email from Grant Us Funding to reset your password. (RS NOTE: i think most forgot password reset emails use a link in an email to verify? not sure).


What if the email address on my account was also changed?


If both your email address and password have been changed, you may need additional help from us.

If you haven't yet done so, contact us by going to the contact us link in the header on our website. (Note: Be sure to let us know that you've already tried each of the steps listed in this article. We'll then do our best to assist.)


What should I do if I suspect an unauthorized person accessed my Grant Us Funding account?


If you suspect that an unauthorized person may have accessed your Grant Us Funding account, take the following precautions as soon as possible:

  • Change your Grant Us Funding password on GrantUsFunding.com. Be sure to use a strong password that you don't use for any other service.
  • Change the password to the email account you use for your Grant Us Funding account. Also, consider setting up two-step verification, which adds an extra layer of security to your account even if your password is somehow compromised.

How Grant Us Funding keeps your files secure


Grant Us Funding houses your valuable searches and keeps track of your saved grant files. To maintain safety, Grant Us Funding is designed with multiple layers of protection, distributed across a scalable, secure infrastructure. These layers of protection include:

  • Grant Us Funding files at rest are encrypted using 256-bit Advanced Encryption Standard (AES)
  • Grant Us Funding uses Secure Sockets Layer (SSL)/Transport Layer Security (TLS) to protect data in transit between Grant Us Funding apps and our servers.
  • SSL/TSL creates a secure tunnel protected by 128-bit or higher Advanced Encryption Standard (AES) encryption.
  • Grant Us Funding applications and infrastructure are regularly tested for security vulnerabilities, and hardened to enhance security and protect against attacks.
  • Two-step verification is available for an extra layer of security at login.
  • If you use two-step verification, you can choose to receive security codes by text message or from an authenticator app.
  • Public files are only viewable by people who have a link to the file(s). This will only happen if your share a link to your data.

Access to users’ file content is limited to a small number of people. We have strict policy and technical access controls that prohibit access to file content except in these rare circumstances. In addition, we use a number of physical and electronic security measures to protect user information from unauthorized access.

Use of Cookies and similar technologies


This article explains how we use technologies like cookies and pixel tags.


Cookies

Cookies are small data files sent to your browser when you visit a site. We use both our own cookies, as well as third-party cookies, to do a few different things:

  • Log you in to our services
  • Remember preferences and settings
  • Keep your account secure
  • Better understand how people are using our services and how we can improve, customize, and promote those services based on that information

To promote our services, we use third-party service providers, like Facebook, Google and LinkedIn, that may set cookies on your device when you visit our pages. This helps them understand your advertising preferences so they can show you ads that are more relevant to your interests.


Pixels

A pixel tag ("pixel") is a small piece of code that can be embedded on websites and emails. We use pixels and cookies to learn how you interact with our webpages and emails. This information helps us, and the advertising services providers we use, better promote our services. For example, if you visit one of our Grant Us Funding pages, you may later see an ad for Grant Us Funding when you visit a tech news site.


OPT-OUT and DNT

You can set your browser to not accept cookies, but this may limit your ability to use the Services. Team Admins

Change Admin rights for your Grant Us Funding Team

On a Grant Us Funding Team, there are Admins and Team Members. Admins can manage membership to the team, change settings, and manage billing. Grant Us Funding recommends that all Grant Us Funding paid accounts have at least two Admins. The Team Admin has global rights and the Co-Admin/s will be assigned their roles by the Team Admin. If you’re a solo team, you are automatically assigned as Team Admin and will not need additional members to start your subscription.

Admins on a paid Grant Us Funding plan will have global permissions (as a team Admin) or a more limited set of permissions (as user Co-Admin). The Co-Admin’s roles will be assigned by the Team Admin.


How to Add an additional Admin:

  1. Log in to GrantUsFunding.com
  2. In your Portal, go to your User page and to the Team Management section.
  3. If the user you’d like to add Admin permissions to is not already Invited to your Team, use the Invite form to initiate the process. Once they accept and register, they will be added to your Team.
  4. In the Team Management section, select “Admin” in the dropdown box next to their name.
  5. Click the Save Changes button.

How to Transfer an Admin role/downgrade from an Admin to another role:

Grant Us Funding requires every Team to have at least one Admin. Before you change your role from Admin to Team Member, please make sure another User on your Team is also assigned to the Admin role. If there is not another User assigned to this role, you will not be able to proceed with the role change.

  1. Log in to GrantUsFunding.com
  2. In your Portal, go to your User page and to the Team Management section.

  6. Click the Save button.

What is NLP?

What is Natural Language Processing?

Have you ever wondered how smart technology can really get, given that the majority of human knowledge is contained within the written word?

Take the study of Linguistics and combine it with Computer Science. Add a dash of Information Engineering and some Artificial Intelligence, and you get Natural Language Processing.

It’s all about programming software to process and analyze large amounts of Natural Language data, and make that information available and useful with context.


So, what does this have to do with grants?

Digital information is only useful when we derive an understanding of the interactions, the context, and then make those translations understood.

When you look for a grant match, it’s essential to be able to describe your project in detail. Generally, the more specific your work is to the intent of the grantor, the higher the potential for funding.


How our Grant Matching Works

When combined with your starting Keywords, the NLP-enabled system provides a shortcut between you and the funding you need.

Our Data Search returns suggested Data Keywords relevant to your starting input. Don’t waste time combing through endless lists and fields; with our software, you’re directed straight to additional Keywords that are found in similar grants. Then we focus your results, maximizing your opportunities for successful funding. And this is only the start of our smarter technology.


Grant Probability Score

Your Grant Probability Score (GPS) is based on a multiple factors. These include your keyword matches, organizations qualifications along with our algorithmic determinations. This takes into account the potential amounts of funding, timing and a host of other considerations.


Why is this important?

Your time and effort should be focused on the highest potentials for success. The funding opportunities that you and the grantor are as close to in sync as possible.
